First Year Engineering Physics Study Material
Unit 1 Acoustics, Ultrasonic Waves and Dielectric

Acoustics:

Introduction, Reflection of sound(reverberation and echo), absorption coefficient, Sabine's formula, Acoustical planing of building and factors affecting architectural acoustics of building.



Ultrasonic Waves:

Properties, Production of Ultrasonic waves: Piezoelectric method, Application(Any three in detail).



Dielectric:

Polar and non-polar Dielectric, Polarization, Types of Dielectric polarization.

Unit 2 Engineering Optics

Engineering optics:

Interference in thin film due to reflected light, Wedge shaped film, Newton Rings, Applications, Polarization: Introduction, types of polarization, definition of optical activity & specific rotation, Lasers: Characteristics, spontaneous emission and stimulated ermission; metastable state, population inversion, types of pumping, resonant cavity, He-Ne Laser, semiconductor laser, Applications of Lasers, Optical fibre: Acceptance cone, Numerical aperture, applications of fibre optics

Unit 3 Quantum Mechanics

Quantum Mechanics:

De- Broglie hypothesis of matter waves, Wave function and its physical significance, Heisenberg's uncertainty principle and its application, Schrodinger's time dependent wave equation, Schrodinger's time independent wave equation, Introduction to quantum computing (bits & qubits, difference between classical and quantum computers).

Unit 4 Crystal Structure and Nuclear Physics

Crystal Structure:

Fundamental concepts (lattice, basis, umit cell, crystal systems), Cabic structure: Number of atoms per unit cell, atomic radius, co-ordination number, packing fraction, Comparison of Aluminum (FCC) and Iron (BCC) at room temperature, Miller indices, Relation berween 'p' and 'a'.



Nuclear Physics:

Introduction to mass defect, Q value of maclear reaction, properties of a. B and y rays, GM Counter

Unit 5 Physics of Advanced Materials

Magnetic Materials:

Types of magnetic materials, magnetic domain and hysteresis curve.



Semiconductor:

Conductivity of semiconductors, Hall Effect (derivation & Applications).



Super Conductor:

Definition, critical temperature, critical magnetic field, Meissner effect, type 1 & II superconductors, Introduction to BCS theory.



Nanomaterials:

Introduction, top-down and bottom-up approach, Introduction to XRD, FESEM, VSM, and CNT, Applications of nano-materials.
